I'm Robert Contant. I write here about the parts of cybersecurity I actually
enjoy practicing: hardening real systems, detection engineering, OSINT, building
AI-assisted security workflows, and the home lab that ties it all together.
This is where I document what I'm building and what I'm learning - in
public, in my own words.

This site sits alongside my GitHub on my resume. I'm a hands-on security
engineer who is also fluent in governance and compliance, and I think both
halves of that story matter: the building, and the discipline to prove the
building actually works. You should be able to see both from these pages.

## What I write about

A few themes will come up again and again here, because they're what I spend my
time on.

### Securing and hardening real environments

Practical security work on the systems I can write about - hardening home
and small-business networks, locking down Microsoft 365 and Azure, tightening
identity and access. Less theory, more &ldquo;here's what I changed and why.&rdquo;
That includes the unglamorous home-front version: hardening and monitoring my
own home network, which is a surprisingly good proving ground for ideas before
they touch anything that matters.

### Detection, response, and the Microsoft toolchain

Notes from the home lab and from work I can share safely - building
detections, investigating suspicious activity, and getting real value out of the
Microsoft security stack for incident response. There's a lot of capability in
those tools that goes unused because nobody writes down how to actually drive
them during an investigation. I'll write some of that down.

### AI infrastructure and agents

I'm building a personal AI infrastructure - inspired in part by Daniel
Miessler's work on the idea - and a growing set of purpose-built AI agents
for security and operations tasks. I'll write about what I'm building, which use
cases actually pay off, and which ones turn out to be more trouble than they're
worth. My view on AI in security is practical, not hype-driven, and I'd rather
show the real results than the demo.

### Automation and scripts

Small tools that remove repetitive manual work - for example, scripts to
provision new users and assign them to the right groups in Entra ID or Active
Directory. The kind of thing that saves ten minutes a hundred times. I'll share
the approach and the lessons, sanitized so they're useful without being a
copy-paste liability.

### Learning out loud: labs, tools, and techniques

I learn by doing - penetration testing labs, vulnerability scanning, new
tools and techniques, and the lessons that only show up when you actually run
the thing. Expect short writeups when something surprises me, wins and losses
both. The losses are usually where the lesson is.

### OSINT

Tooling and techniques I build and explore for myself - with the
obligatory reminder not to point any of this at people you don't have permission
to investigate. The ethics aren't a footnote; they're part of the discipline.

### Compliance, translated

A lot of my background is in GRC, and one thing I keep running into is that
frameworks like SOC&nbsp;2 and ISO&nbsp;27001 get written in a language that
engineers, IT, and DevOps teams don't speak. So I'll translate: what a given
control actually means in a modern SaaS environment, and what &ldquo;satisfying&rdquo;
it looks like in terms of the systems and workflows people already use -
not a binder of policies, but real configuration and real evidence.
