I'm Bobby Contant - a cybersecurity and information assurance professional
focused on hands-on security engineering. I spend my time hardening systems,
investigating risk, automating the tedious parts of security work, and building
tools that make security more practical and more measurable. I'm equally
comfortable talking to auditors, engineers, and executives, but I'm most
energized when I'm actually improving a system rather than just documenting it.

## Where I'm headed

My background spans security and compliance leadership for SaaS and cloud-based
companies - ISO&nbsp;27001, SOC&nbsp;1, SOC&nbsp;2, ISAE&nbsp;3402, GDPR,
NIS&nbsp;2, vendor risk, audit readiness, and security operations oversight.
That foundation taught me how security programs are built, measured, tested,
and improved. But my focus now is moving deeper into the engineering side:
cloud security, Microsoft 365 and Azure hardening, identity and access
management, detection and response, vulnerability management, and security
automation. I want to spend more time securing systems directly and less time
describing them.

## How I work

A theme that runs through everything I do is turning complexity into clarity.
Security, compliance, and IT have a way of fragmenting across tools, tickets,
spreadsheets, documents, and audits - and a lot of value gets lost in the
gaps. I like building the repeatable processes, dashboards, and workflows that
pull that mess back together, so an organization can move from reactive
security toward something proactive and measurable. I try to explain technical
topics in plain language without losing the substance, and to translate
abstract security requirements into steps that engineering, IT, and leadership
can actually act on.

## AI and automation

I'm genuinely interested in where AI fits into the future of cybersecurity, IT
operations, and OSINT - but my view is practical, not hype-driven. AI
should help people make better decisions, cut repetitive manual work, surface
useful insights, and make expert-level workflows accessible to smaller teams
that don't have large security budgets. I experiment constantly: using AI to
analyze security data, support audits, review policies, evaluate SOC
performance, and build smarter workflows. A lot of what shows up in my projects
and on this blog comes out of that experimentation.

## Outside the day job

I'm a builder by instinct. I run a home lab and self-hosted infrastructure
where I test ideas, break things on purpose, and learn by doing -
detection engineering, SIEM work, identity labs, AI assistants, OSINT tooling,
and privacy-respecting local-first systems. I'm drawn to work that helps small
and mid-sized organizations get effective security without unnecessary
complexity, because I don't think good security should be reserved for
enterprises with big budgets. I also have prior experience in the U.S. Marine
